Additional Notes

Best Traditional Rope Feel

  • Jute and Hemp are the most traditional choices for shibari.
  • Both provide excellent friction, knot security, and tactile feedback.

Best Beginner-Friendly Rope

  • Cotton → Softest and easiest on skin
  • Posh / Spun Polyester → Easier maintenance with suspension capability
  • MFP → Affordable practice rope

Best for Suspension

  • Jute
  • Hemp
  • Posh / Spun Polyester
  • Specialized Nylon or Static Climbing Rope setups

Highest Maintenance

  1. Jute
  2. Hemp
  3. Coconut Rope

Lowest Maintenance

  1. MFP
  2. Nylon
  3. Spun Polyester
  4. Paracord

Rope Safety Guidelines

Rope play can be a rewarding and creative practice, but it requires awareness, communication, and respect for physical limits. Understanding anatomy and risk areas is essential to reduce the chance of injury.

High-Risk Areas (“Danger Zones”)

Extra caution should always be taken around areas where nerves and blood vessels are close to the surface or easily compressed:

  • Wrists - high risk of nerve compression and circulation restriction
  • Elbows - vulnerable ulnar nerve area (“funny bone” region)
  • Upper arms - risk of radial and ulnar nerve pressure
  • Hips - potential for joint strain and circulation restriction
  • Upper thighs - major nerve pathways and blood flow sensitivity
  • Knees - joint stress and ligament strain risk

Warning Signs to Watch For

  • Numbness or “pins and needles” sensation
  • Sharp, radiating, or electric pain
  • Loss of strength or inability to move a limb normally
  • Skin turning pale, blue, or unusually cold
  • Swelling or persistent discomfort after release

Core Safety Rules

  • Never tie alone - always have a present, attentive partner or safety observer
  • Never tie the neck - avoid any pressure or suspension around the throat
  • Check circulation frequently - loosen immediately if there is any concern
  • Communicate continuously - use clear verbal or agreed non-verbal signals

Essential Safety Tools

  • Safety shears - keep within immediate reach at all times
  • Quick-release knowledge - ensure knots can be undone rapidly if needed
  • Backup lighting (for low-light scenes)

Aftercare & Monitoring

After any rope session, check for lingering numbness, bruising, or joint discomfort. Some nerve issues can present delayed symptoms, so continued observation after the session is important.
